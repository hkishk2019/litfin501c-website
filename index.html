About the Summit

Where Law, Finance, and Innovation Converge

The Chicago Litigation Finance Summit brings together the industry's most influential voices — academic scholars, funders, investors, judges, attorneys, and policy advocates — for an unprecedented day of insight and dialogue at the Museum of Contemporary Art.

This fully non-profit, student-run event is organized by students from Northwestern University and University of Chicago. CLE credits would be available for panelists and attendees.*

Why Attend

Six Reasons This Conference
Is Built for You

A once-a-year gathering designed for impact, substance, and lasting connections.

01
The entire industry — one room, one day.
This conference brings together policymakers, academic scholars, litigation funders, deal advisors, insurance providers, market liquidation providers, Am Law 100 attorneys, legal tech innovators, and Fortune 500 general counsels — the leading players across every link in the legal finance value chain — for a day of candid, high-level conversation you simply cannot replicate through separate meetings.
02
Tackle the hard questions head-on.
The panels go beyond surface-level overviews. Expect rigorous discussion of the industry's most challenging issues and emerging opportunities — from regulatory headwinds and disclosure debates to new asset classes and cross-border structures — with a focus on finding practical, win-win strategies that move the field forward.
03
Shape the future of the industry together.
Legal finance is at an inflection point. This gathering is designed to unify voices across the ecosystem — funders, lawyers, academics, regulators, and technologists — to build consensus, establish best practices, and advance the responsible integration and development of the industry as a force for good.
04
Earn CLE credits from the people who are defining the field.
Every panel is built for substance. The content is pragmatic, current, and delivered by practitioners and scholars who are actively shaping legal finance law, policy, and practice. Walk away with actionable insights and continuing legal education credit.
05
Build relationships that last.
The best deals, collaborations, and ideas start with a conversation over coffee or a handshake at the reception. The conference is structured to maximize networking time so you leave with new connections — and new friends.
06
Experience Chicago at its finest.
The conference is held at one of Chicago's premier museum venues. Take a private tour of world-class exhibits, then step outside into the city's legendary summer — lakefront views, architecture, and dining that make the trip worthwhile even before the first panel begins.
